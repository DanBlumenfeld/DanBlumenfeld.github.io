--- layout: layouts/base.njk title: Candied Citrus Peel ---

This is a simple three-step process: blanch, candy, and roll in sugar. Depending on the thickness of the peels, the number of times to blanch or duration spent candying in syrup may vary widely.

Ingredients

  • Fresh citrus fruit (grapefruit, orange, lemon, and lime all work, with adjustment to cooking times)
  • granulated sugar (lots)

Instructions

  1. Carefully remove the peels and cut them into similarly sized pieces, roughly 1/4" thick. I usually halve the fruit and juice them, then use a spoon to remove any remaining pulp or flesh before cutting.
  2. Blanch the cut peels in boiling water for 5 minutes 2-5 times, to soften them and remove some of the sharpness. For thin soft peels (e.g. mandarin oranges), I'll blanch twice; for thick peels (e.g. grapefruit), I'll blanch 4 or 5 times. Most fruit are good with 3 times.
  3. For candying, make a simple syrup with rough 1:1 proportions of sugar and water, enough to keep the peels well covered. Heat until the sugar dissolves and it's starting to boil.
  4. Add the blanched peels to the syrup and bring to a boil, then simmer for 60-90 minutes, taking more time for thicker peels.
  5. After the candying is complete, drain the syrup (reserving it for other purposes such as making cocktails!), then spread the peels out on a baking rack in an even layer, being sure they aren't touching one another. Allow to dry for 1-2 hours, until just slightly tacky to the touch.
  6. Roll each piece of peel in sugar, rearranging them on a clean rack afterwards. Allow to dry for at least an hour, if not overnight, before packaging.
  7. Store in an airtight container at room temperature, or freeze for later use.
